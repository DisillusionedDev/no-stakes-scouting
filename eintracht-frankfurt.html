1. Tactical Breakdown: Lateral Deficits in Build-Up Phases

Eintracht Frankfurt's defensive structure thrives on trapping opponents along the touchlines. Their tactical setup uses aggressive wing-backs to cut off early escape passes, forcing opposing midfielders into dense central pockets where their double-pivot can execute physical turnovers.

However, data shows a profound vulnerability when their high-press line is bypassed using direct, diagonal switches. Because their central midfielders push exceptionally high to support the initial squeeze, a rapid structural tilt exposes vast horizontal spaces on the opposite flank. A designated deep anchor capable of securing these vacant channels immediately blunts Frankfurt's transition efficiency.
