The System Flaw: Midfield Pivot Abandonment

While Manchester United's attacking metrics show immense fluidity under Michael Carrick, their out-of-possession structure remains highly vulnerable. Carrick prefers a rigid 4-4-2 defensive mid-block configuration.

When opponents build out with a dynamic three-man midfield, United's front pairing focuses entirely on sealing passing lanes to the center-backs. This leaves the opponent's lone deep pivot completely free. As the opposition progresses, **Kobbie Mainoo is regularly isolated in direct 2v1 central overloads**, forcing him to abandon his zone to close down space and leaving the defensive line entirely unprotected against central transition runs.
